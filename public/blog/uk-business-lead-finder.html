Looking for a reliable way to find UK business leads? Here's everything you need to know about sourcing, filtering and contacting B2B prospects in the UK.

Finding UK Business Leads: Your Options

There are more ways to find UK business leads than ever — but the quality and efficiency of each method varies enormously. Understanding your options helps you build the most effective prospecting system.

Automated Lead Finder Tools

The most efficient method for volume prospecting. Tools like ZapLead pull UK business data automatically based on your search criteria — business type, location, size. You get a list of relevant prospects in minutes rather than hours.

Google Maps

An underrated source of UK business leads. Searching for a business type in a specific location surfaces relevant businesses with contact information and website links. It's manual and time-consuming at scale, but useful for targeted research on specific prospects.

Companies House

Lists all registered UK companies with director names, registered addresses, and filing history. Free and comprehensive but requires significant manual effort. More useful for research on specific companies than for building prospect lists at scale.

LinkedIn

Good for identifying decision-makers at specific companies, but slow for building large lists from scratch.

Most efficient approach: Use an automated tool to build your initial prospect list, then use LinkedIn and Companies House to research the most promising prospects before reaching out.

What to Do With Your Lead List

  • Prioritisation — score leads based on fit with your ideal customer profile
  • Contact enrichment — find the right person to contact, not just the general enquiries email
  • Outreach sequence — a consistent process for contacting and following up
  • Tracking — know where every prospect is in your pipeline at all times

GDPR Considerations

UK GDPR applies to B2B email marketing, but the rules are less restrictive than for consumer marketing. Cold emailing businesses is permitted under legitimate interest, provided the contact is relevant to their business and you include a clear opt-out. Always check the latest ICO guidelines before running large campaigns.
